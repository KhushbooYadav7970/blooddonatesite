Types of Donation

Packed Red Blood Cells

What is it?

Blood collected straight from the donor into a blood bag and mixed with an anticoagulant is called whole blood. This collected whole blood is then centrifuged and red cell, platelets, and plasma are separated. The separated red cells are mixed with a preservative to be called as packed red blood cells.

Plasma

What is it?

Plasma is the liquid portion of the blood that remains when the blood cells are removed. It is collected using a special machine that separates the plasma and returns the rest of the blood to the donor.

Platelet

What is it?

Platelets are tiny cell fragments that help your body form clots to stop bleeding. They are collected through a process called apheresis, which separates the platelets from the rest of the blood components.
