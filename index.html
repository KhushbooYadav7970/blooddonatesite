Types of Donation

Packed Red Blood Cells

Packed Red Blood Cells (PRBCs) are whole blood units that have been centrifuged to separate red blood cells from plasma and platelets, then mixed with a preservative. They are used to treat severe anemia and blood loss in medical settings.

Plasma

Plasma is the liquid component of blood that remains after red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets are removed. It contains proteins, electrolytes, hormones, and waste products, and is crucial for clotting and immune function in the body.

Platelet

Platelets are tiny cell fragments that help your body form clots to stop bleeding.
